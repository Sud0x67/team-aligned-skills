# Equity Research Source Quality

Use primary, dated evidence before commentary.

## Source Hierarchy

- Highest confidence: company filings, audited financials, exchange filings, regulator databases, investor relations materials.
- High confidence: earnings call transcripts, official presentations, reputable financial databases.
- Medium confidence: Reuters, Bloomberg, FT, WSJ, recognized industry reports, sell-side research.
- Low confidence: social posts, forums, unsourced summaries, scraped aggregator pages.

## Freshness Rules

- Current price, market cap, analyst estimates, guidance, ratings, and news must be checked live.
- Financial statements should match the latest filed period.
- Peer multiples must use the same date and metric definition.
- If source dates conflict, show the conflict instead of smoothing it away.

## Evidence Labels

Label every major claim as one of:

- Fact: directly observed from a source.
- Calculation: derived from stated inputs.
- Inference: analyst judgment based on facts.
- Assumption: forward-looking or uncertain input.
